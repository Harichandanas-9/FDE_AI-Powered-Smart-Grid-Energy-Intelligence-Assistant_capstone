AI-Powered Smart Grid Energy Intelligence Assistant 

Overview 

Modern power utilities operate large-scale electrical grids consisting of transformers, substations, smart meters, transmission systems, renewable energy assets, and distribution infrastructure. These systems continuously generate telemetry data, outage records, voltage measurements, consumption patterns, and operational alarms. 

Grid operators and utility engineers often spend significant time identifying failures because operational information is distributed across SCADA systems, smart meter infrastructure, maintenance logs, outage management systems, and monitoring platforms. 

Engineers frequently investigate situations such as: 

"Voltage instability is increasing across multiple distribution zones."  

"Transformer overload conditions are recurring during peak demand."  

"Unexpected power fluctuations observed after maintenance activity."  

"Renewable energy variability causing grid balancing issues."  

"Smart meter anomalies detected in a residential service area."  

Traditional monitoring systems rely heavily on threshold alarms and manual analysis, increasing operational complexity and outage recovery time. 

The system should help utility operators retrieve historical grid incidents, identify probable causes, recommend mitigation actions, and generate explainable grid intelligence. 

 

Objective 

The objective of this project is to build an AI-powered smart grid energy intelligence assistant that enables utility engineers to investigate power system issues using natural-language queries and intelligent grid analysis. 

The system should retrieve historical operational incidents, correlate telemetry patterns, detect abnormal grid behavior, and generate explainable root-cause insights to improve grid reliability and operational efficiency.
